VeCheck

VeCheck (Vehicle Check) is an innovative vehicle health check inspection platform that helps businesses increase sales and build stronger connections with their customers.

Role:
Product Designer
Tools:
Adobe XD, Figma
Timeline:
2021 - Ongoing

Overview

VeCheck solves some of the major problems faced in the garage. Its aim is to maintain a smooth workflow by enabling managers to create customizable checklists for all services provided by the garage. Mechanics can then follow this checklist during inspections.

My main duties were to design the web application for the admin role and the mobile app for the mechanic role from scratch and created various visuals as needed. I also worked on improving the user experience of the final product by introducing a more user-friendly interface and additional features.

In translating the inspection checklist form from paper to screen, I aimed at creating an engaging user-friendly checklist and additional features that can be customized based on the user needs.

Challange

The most common car problems are broken windshield wipers, broken brakes, misaligned wheels, and burned-out lights.

When the customer has handed in the car for service, they can feel sure that their car is in safe hands. But it is not always so easy to explain to a customer if, for example, you discover a fault that must be fixed while the car is on the lift can be challenging.

How might we... create an easy workflow for inspectors and customers so that more efficient checks are made in a way that nothing is missed?

Key needs

The insights and needs I identified from the research and feedback that I received played a crucial role in how I designed our solution and as a result I discovered the following points.

Insights

Solution

Vecheck undertakes all kinds of services and additional work. If you inspect your car and find a defect, you can quickly photograph or attach a video of the problem. Add a price and send it directly to your customer. While going through this process we came to some of the key solutions.

Process

In order to design a better journey, we figured out what mediums would be feasible for implementation. Since smartphones are mostly used by anybody, we came to an agreement that the best medium to solve the problems and also allow more flexibility for mechanics/inspectors is a mobile app and for the customers is the SMS link which is sent to the customer to open it in the browser.

For the admin role, since we deal with a lot of data and functions that are used to manage configurations, settings and features, we decided that it is best to have an admin panel in our web application.

Competitive Analysis

To better understand the flow behind digital vehicle inspection, I researched similar platforms to gather different ideas, and I found out that almost none of them had some of the features that we provide. This then led me to contribute to some of the best solutions for our product.

Ideation

After validating our problem statement and pain points, I started ideating different scenarios and use cases for the web application and the mobile app. After that, I was able to put my ideas on the screen and paper where we all brainstormed for several weeks until we got a solid idea. Once I observed everything and clarified my thought process and how the flow started to make more sense, I started with some rough wireframes and began refining them.

Wireframes

From our ideation, we consolidated our ideas and afterwards I created the wireframes and prototypes our ideas. Our first round was to test different versions and approaches that I had created at a low-fi and in the other versions I created more enhanced versions since we were looking to gather more detailed feedback from users. As we started to have more features, the design took shape both in terms of the interface and user experience.

Takeaways

This project was challenging but also rewarding. It is my first time working on an early-stage startup and the learning curve on this project was extremely steep. Starting everything from scratch was such an eye-opening experience that taught me a lot. Due to the many details that we collected and new features, there were many times that I needed to design and redesign and the time constraint for our prototype was much shorter than the given time for research.

Since this project is in development and we are adding more features into the product, my focus will be more on user testing by gathering users feedback more than I did in the beginning.
